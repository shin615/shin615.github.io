Aclara: Profield

Providing various smart solutions across the country

Overview

Aclara works to provide smart infrastructure products such as smart meters across the country. During my time at this company as a UX Design Intern I designed a self service appointment portal that could be used by Aclara’s customers to make appointments for meter replacement. In addition, I helped implement a UI refresh of the internal software used by the company and helped implement gamification for the field technicians in order to improve efficiency and productivity. However, I will only be able to share about the customer portal to respect company policy.

Project Details

Project Length

13 weeks

Project Role

UX Design Intern

Deliverables

  • Wireframes
  • Customer Portal Mockup
  • Usability Test Results

Background

When I first arrived at Aclara to begin my UX design internship for the summer I was given the task of developing a web portal that would allow customers to make their own appointments for meter replacement rather than having them all contact the customer service department to get an appointment. To accomplish this I first began by developing a problem statement that would help guide my design process.

Problem Statement

Aclara’s Operations Team is focused on increasing the site visit success rate; however, due to the rising call volume at the call center, the operations team has been struggling to schedule appointments, which makes the smart meter installation process less efficient.

Initial Research & Brainstorm

Because I had no prior knowledge of Aclara’s procedures for their interactions with their customers I had to do some initial research by interviewing some of the employees who had a better sense of that area. So, along with the UX Research Intern, I reached out and interviewed the Quality Assurance (QA) Manager, Customer Service Rep. (CSR) Manager, and Field Technician Support. From this we were able to get an understanding of how we usually contact customers about service appointments and what problems prevented technicians from completing jobs.

With this new information in mind I began brainstorming various features that this customer portal might have:

Iterations

Using the ideas I had brainstormed I began sketching out and wireframing various designs that could potentially become the customer portal.

I then met with the software development manager, VP of technology, and the UX Designer to get feedback on my initial designs. During this meeting I was told that I would have to take off many of the functions that I had added because they wanted this to be a simple interface that the customer could finish quickly

After this meeting I created a digital wireframe that took into account the feedback I had reieved incorporating ways for the customer to add personal notes and pictures while making an appointment.

Then after finishing a wireframe of the web version of the customer portal I met again with the developers to get more feedback From this meeting I learned that we would want to make this portal responsive and have a mobile version as well. So I developed new wireframes for the mobile and web versions of the portal that incorporated all the feedback I recieved. One of the largest being that all the appointment details should be decided on one page.

I then received feedback on the new wireframes from the UX Architect and used it to create high fidelity mockups of the mobile version which we utilized in the usability tests.

Usability Tests

Working with the UX Research Intern, I helped develop a series of questions and scenarios that would help us test the usability of the mobile version of the Customer Portal. Because we did not have a large budget we conducted usability tests on Aclara employees, however we made sure to only test those that were not software developers who were aware of the product so that they would be unbiased in their opinions.

Final Iteration & Design Approval

Finally after completing those usability tests I incorporated the feedback I received and created another iteration. With this iteration finished I made a presentation of the designs to the VP of Software and the Product Owner. During my presentation they provided me with additional feedback along with the approval of the design. After this I made presented the design one more time to the operations team who approved the design for development.

Reflection

During my experience at Aclara I was able to learn:

  • How to manage my time to meet company deadlines
  • How to gather and present research in an easy to understand format
  • How to communicate to different kinds of stakeholders
  • How to balance different needs and requirements set by different stakeholders while designing a real product
  • how the user centered design process and UX Design in general is utilized in a company setting
