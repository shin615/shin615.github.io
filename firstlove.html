First Love
Retreat

A branding project for Seattle Onnuri Church
& Seattle Bridge Church's annual Youth Winter Retreat

Overview

Every year Seattle Onnuri Church and Seattle Bridge Church come together to host a 3-4 day Winter Retreat for its Youth Students. For 2019, I decided to volunteer for the design team and help them develop the theme and decorations for the event.

Project Details

Project Length

12 weeks

Project Role

Visual Designer

Deliverables

  • Two Main Logos
  • Sub-Icons
  • Day Specific Logos
  • Shirt Designs
  • Pen Design
  • Event Slide Deck
  • Booklet
  • Physical Decorations

Getting context

Even before I became a part of the design team for the event the theme had been developed to a certain extent. The general message and goal for each individual day and the event as a whole had been developed. So, when the other volunteers and I joined it was about familiarizing ourselves with what had been created and how that might be translated into visual design.

Research & Planning

In order to create a design we first had to choose a user. For me and my team we chose to design a product for Librarians. This is because libraries are public places so we would have easy access to both the user group and a potential problem space. After choosing our user group we began researching librarians so that we could learn more about the different problems they face in doing their job. So each member of our team conducted interviews with different librarians from both local and metropolitan libraries using a general set of questions we developed together. I personally interviewed a research specialist from the local Lynnwood library. Then, we synthesized the data by implementing an affinity diagarm and used our findings to develop a user journey map and user personas to help guide us throughout the rest of the design process.

Ideation

Having collected a good amount of data and created personas and journey maps my team and I went back to analyze the data one more time to form a design question and design requirements that would help guide our work. Then we started ideating by each choosing two of the design requirements and using them to create storyboards so that we could better visualize and understand each membersâ€™ ideas. My storyboards outlined two different products; one, was a translated resource booklet which would help those who did not speak english learn how to utilize different resources offered by the library, the second, details a remote help desk which would allow librarians to aid patrons who cannot physically come to the library. After creating the storyboards, we revised the design question and requirements to better fit the idea we chose to focus on. My team and I used the revised question and requirements to create the Information Architecture for our product.

With a strong framework in place for our product we created a paper prototype so that we could evaluate the strengths and weakness of our product with members of our user group So I went and made contact with librarians from various departments within the University of Washington until I had three volunteers who help me and my team conduct usability tests with our paper prototype.

Implementation

From the usability tests we were able to gather valuable feedback about our initial design My team and I then created annotated wireframes that incorporated the feedback we received. We then shared these wireframes in class with the other teams so that we could gather more feedback on our design. We then used the feedback from class to create high fidelity mockups which we presented to the class along with a panel of UX Designers from companies such as Google and Amazon.

Reflection

Through this project I was able to strengthen a number of different skills:

  • Working with other designers to create a product that we agreed upon
  • Coordinating and conducting meetings with members of the user group for research
  • Managing time in order to meet design deadlines and goals the team set for ourselves
