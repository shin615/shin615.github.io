SOARING Project

Working to understand how older
adults manage health information

Overview

As a part of the Autumn Directed Research Group for the SOARING Project I worked with Associate Professor Julie Kientz and Graduate Student Dawn Sakaguchi-Tang to ideate and test various solutions for how health information management could be improved for older patients.

Project Details

Project Length

10 weeks

Project Role

UX Researcher

Deliverables

  • Low Fidelity Sketches
  • Wireframes
  • Demo Video

Background

The Directed Research Groups program is a part of the Human Centered Design and Engineering Department where students can apply for and join groups dedicated to aiding department faculty with their research. Through my participation in these groups I was able to not only hone UX research skills that I had learned in class but also explore new UX research topics.

The overall goal of the SOARING project is to better understand how older health patients manage their health information and how various stakeholders such as caregivers and family members play a part in helping manage that information.

Our goal within the research group was to help expand on the data already collected by creating designs and conducting usability tests/design sessions with older health patients. Additionally another goal, if time permitted, was to create a design book including the designs made by the research group to help inform future designers on this subject.

Research

By the time that our research group became a part of the project some preliminary research had been completed by a previous directed research group by conducting interviews with the user group and different stakeholders. The previous DRG had also developed personas using the data collected, which the team and I were able to use in ideating new designs.

Ideation

With the personas in mind the team and I began to ideate potential solutions to alleviate their pain points. After a few ideation sessions we studied what commonalities these design solutions had and used them to form design requirements. Each member of the team was then assigned a requirement; I was assigned the accessibility requirement then assigned into a sub-group with the members that were assigned the usability and customization design requirements.

Development

Together we designed a toolbar that could be utilized to help patients navigate within the hospital portals First we developed a rough wireframe of the product, then gained feedback from the other members of the DRG.

Then we created a mockup of the design and incorporated it into a video presentation that we used to gain feedback from residents of nursing homes in the area.
